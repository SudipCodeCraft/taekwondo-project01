Poomsae

跆拳道 Poomsae (sometimes spelled Pumsae), also known as Taekwondo forms or patterns, is a fundamental aspect of Taekwondo training. Here's a breakdown of the concept:

Series of Movements: It's a sequence of Taekwondo techniques linked together into a structured pattern.

Training Purposes: Poomsae serves various purposes. It helps refine basic techniques and movements, building and solidifying essential Taekwondo skills.

Grading System: It's a primary method for evaluating students' progress. As they advance through ranks, they learn and perform more complex poomsae.

There are two main categories of poomsae:

Taegeuk Forms (Il Jang to Pal Jang): These are the foundational forms, typically learned by beginners and lower-ranked students. There are eight Taegeuk forms, each with a symbolic meaning.

Black Belt Forms: These are more complex patterns learned by higher-ranked students (black belts and above). They require greater physical and mental effort compared to Taegeuk forms.
